## 1. Introduction to the CES Heatmap

The **Capital Efficiency Score (CES)** is the principal observable in Economic Geometry, measuring the overall viability of a business configuration. This document provides a complete heatmap of CES values for all 64 states in Ω, visualized in multiple formats to reveal patterns, clusters, and gradients.

**Definition Reminder:**
```
CES(ω) = (Fₗ(a) × M_v(v) × Pₜ(t)) - SF(ω)
```
normalized to [0,100].

## 9. CES Formula Reference

For quick calculation:

```
CES_raw = Fₗ(a) × M_v(v) × Pₜ(t)
CES = 100 × (CES_raw - SF(ω)) / 1.44
```

**Component Values:**
| Agency | Fₗ | Vector | M_v | Phase | Pₜ |
|--------|-----|--------|-----|-------|-----|
| ME (10) | 0.7 | EAST (10) | 1.2 | SPRING (10) | 0.9 |
| WE (11) | 1.0 | SOUTH (11) | 1.0 | SUMMER (11) | 1.2 |
| YOU (01) | 0.8 | WEST (01) | 1.1 | AUTUMN (01) | 1.0 |
| THEY (00) | 0.9 | NORTH (00) | 0.8 | WINTER (00) | 0.6 |

**SF(ω)** ranges from 0.0 to 1.0, typically:
- Ideal states: 0.1-0.2
- Normal states: 0.2-0.4
- Stressed states: 0.4-0.6
- Critical states: 0.6-1.0
